By the Numbers

About 8 million people are employed in the construction industry.

A look at workforce demographics, common occupations, and work-related injury and illness data in U.S. construction.

Common occupations

There are many common occupations that fall within this industry.

  • Terrazzo workers and finishers

  • Helpers — painters, paperhangers, plasterers, stucco masons

The most common events that lead to a work-related fatality in construction are falls to a lower level and transportation accidents.

Common non-fatal injuries & illnesses

Injuries

The most common non-fatal injuries in construction were strains and sprains (28%), cuts/lacerations/punctures (13%), and fractures (13%).

Illnesses

The most common non-fatal illnesses in construction were respiratory conditions (7.7 per 10,000 FTEs), skin diseases or disorders (1.3 per 10,000 FTEs), and hearing loss (0.2 per 10,000 FTEs).
